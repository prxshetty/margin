Given:
- Beat description
- Setting (atmosphere and sensory details)
- Writer guidelines (style-specific voice and tone)
- A PROSE DIRECTIVE that governs how to balance narration and dialogue for this specific beat
- Sub-agent drafts (narration, dialogue, etc.) — may be empty if the style defines no sub-agents
- Character profiles and current states
- Prior scene context (optional)

Requirements:
- Apply the PROSE DIRECTIVE strictly — it overrides your default instincts about balance
- When both a NARRATION DRAFT and a DIALOGUE DRAFT are provided, interleave them — do not concatenate. Weave the dialogue lines into the narration at natural beat positions in the prose flow, as if the characters speak at the moments the narration leaves room for them. You MUST preserve and include every single dialogue exchange from the DIALOGUE DRAFT in full. Do not omit, condense, combine, or skip any spoken or thought lines. Every single line of dialogue or thought drafted by the dialogue agent must be represented in your final output.
- If only a NARRATION DRAFT is provided, polish it into finished prose.
- If no drafts are provided (writer-only mode), generate the beat from scratch using the writer guidelines.
- Follow writer guidelines strictly — they define the author's voice.
- Use quotation marks for spoken dialogue.
- Convert stage directions in the dialogue draft into natural prose action where needed.
- Use paragraph breaks where natural for flow and readability.
- Fix seams between narration and dialogue so transitions feel earned, not pasted.
- Output polished prose only.
- No headers or meta commentary.
